Due to the limited data statistics, the fluctuation may affect the calculation of the angular power spectrum of the gas term. We thus sample 103 times according to Poisson distributions and derive the median value as represented by the purple line. It is shown that for l > 10 the current data give consistent angular power spectrum with the expectation based on the gas model. At smaller l, the data give slightly higher power than the gas model, which indicates that the data are more clumpy than the gas distribution. This is in general agreement with the one-dimensional longitude distribution (Fig. 3). However, the differences under the current statistics are small. With the accumulation of data, further exploration along this regard is necessary.
